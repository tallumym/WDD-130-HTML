Paul McCartney

Sir Paul McCartney, born James Paul McCartney, was born in Liverpool, England, June 18, 1942. He was the bassist in the Beatles, but could sing, play drums, guitar, and many other instruments. He has two siblings named Peter and Ruth.

The first time he met one of his future Beatle mates was at the Liverpool Institute, which was a grammar school. In 1954 he met his schoolmate George Harrsion, who was a year below him. They quickly became friends. Paul McCartney grew up listening to music, his father being a trumpet player and pianist. McCartney enjoyed artists such as Little Richard, Chuck Berry, Elvis Presley, and many others.

George Harrison

George Harrison, born February 25, 1943, in Liverpool, was the lead guitarist in the Beatles, although he could play other instruments such as the sitar, Ukulele, voice, and many others. George was the youngest of four children, sister Louise, and two brothers Harold and Peter. Georges parents were very supportive of his musical ability and dreams, expessialy his mother, Louise, who loved music. Some of Harrison's musical influences included George Formby, Cab Calloway, Django Reinhardt, Carl Perkins, and Slim Whitman.

When Harrison's good friend Paul McCartney joined the Quarymen, Paul tried to get George in the band. But John Lennon being three years older than him, would not let him in the band. Until Paul got George to perform the lead guitar part for the instrumenal "Raunchy", John let him in, still being rude to George because of their age gap.
